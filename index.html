Background information and approach:

  • Delaunay Triangulations

    • Delaunay triangulations maximize the minimum angle of all the angles of the triangles in the triangulation, and tend to avoid sliver triangles.

  • Incremental Method

    • The algorithm is randomized incremental, so it adds the points in random order and it maintains a Delaunay triangulation of the current point set.

Pseudo-code:

DelaunayTriangulation(P)

Input: A set P of n+1 points in the plane.
Output: A Delaunay triangulation of P.
  1. Let p0 be the lexicographically highest point of P, the rightmost among the points with largest y-coordinate.
  2. Let p-1 and p-2 be two points far away and such that P is contained in the triangle p0 p-1 p-2.
  3. Initialize D(P) as the triangulation consisting of the single triangle p0 p-1 p-2.
  4. Compute a random permutation p1, p2, ..., pn of P\{p0}.
  5. for r ← (1 to n) do
  6.   Find a triangle pi pj pk which contains pr
  7.    if (pr lies in the interior of pi pj pk):
  8.      Add edges from pr to the three vertices of pi pj pk, splitting pi pj pk into three triangles.
  9.      ValidEdge(pr, pipj, D(p))
  10.      ValidEdge(pr, pjpk, D(p))
  11.      ValidEdge(pr, pkpi, D(p))
  12.      else: (pr lies on an edge of pi pj pk, say the edge pipj)
  13.      Add edges from pr to pk and to the third vertex pl of the other triangle that is incident to pi pj , splitting the two triangles incident to pi pj into four triangles.
  14.      ValidEdge(pr, pipl, D(p))
  15.      ValidEdge(pr, plpj, D(p))
  16.      ValidEdge(pr, pjpk, D(p))
  17.     ValidEdge(pr, pkpi, D(p))
  18.    end if
  19. end for
  20. Delete all triangles containing p-1 and p-2 as vertices from D(P).
  21. return D(P)

ValidEdge(pr, pipj, D(p))

  1. if pipj is illegal: (Edge pipj is incident to triangles prpipj and pipjpk, and let C be the circle through pi, pj, and pr. The edge pipj is illegal iff the point pk lies in the interior of C.)
  2.    Let pi pj pk be the triangle adjacent to pr pi pj along pi pj
  3.    Replace pipj with prpk
  4.    ValidEdge(pr, pipk, D(p))
  5.    ValidEdge(pr, pkpj, D(p))
  6. end if
